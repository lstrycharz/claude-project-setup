# Research / Analysis — Validation Checklist

Run against the deliverable before sharing. Catches the kinds of issues that make research read as "vibes" rather than "rigorous."

## Sourcing

- [ ] Every non-trivial claim is supported by at least one cited source
- [ ] Single-source claims are explicitly flagged as such
- [ ] Sources are cited with author, date, and publication where available
- [ ] No claims rely on memory or assumption without sourcing

## Methodology

- [ ] Approach is documented — the reader can see how findings were reached
- [ ] Limitations and caveats are acknowledged
- [ ] Sources used vs. sources considered but rejected are noted (if relevant)

## Synthesis quality

- [ ] Contradictions between sources are surfaced, not hidden or averaged
- [ ] Findings distinguish between data, inference, and opinion
- [ ] No weasel words or over-hedging that obscure the actual answer
- [ ] Findings are specific enough to act on

## Structure

- [ ] All required sections from `research-rules.md` are present
- [ ] Question being answered is stated explicitly upfront
- [ ] Open questions / what wasn't answered are listed at the end

## Polish

- [ ] No placeholders remain
- [ ] Voice and tone match `references/global/general-rules.md`
- [ ] Citations are formatted consistently throughout

---

*Adjust to match your team's research standards.*
